Push-Pull-Legs Workout Routine

Primary

  • Latissimus Dorsi (Lats): Main pulling muscle
  • Biceps Brachii: Elbow flexion in pull-ups and chin-ups
  • Trapezius (Traps): Scapular elevation, retraction, stabilization

Secondary

  • Rhomboids: Scapular retraction
  • Brachialis: Assists elbow flexion, especially close grip
  • Brachioradialis: Active in neutral and close grip pulling
  • Rear Deltoids: Shoulder extension support
  • Core: Stabilization in assisted front lever

Stabilizer

  • Forearms: Grip strength and endurance
  • Rotator Cuff: Shoulder joint support

üîµ Pull Workout 2

Primary

  • Latissimus Dorsi (Lats): Pulling strength
  • Trapezius (Traps): Upper and mid-back involvement in rows, pulls
  • Biceps Brachii: Arm flexion in rows and pulldowns

Secondary

  • Rhomboids: Assists in scapular retraction
  • Rear Deltoids: Active in high pulls and rows
  • Brachialis / Brachioradialis: Assist neutral and underhand grips
  • Forearms: Important for grip, curls, and rows
  • Core: Stabilization in deadlifts and rowing movements

Stabilizer

  • Rotator Cuff: Shoulder stabilization during pulling
  • Lower Back: Deadlift support and posture control

üî¥ Push Workout 1

Primary

  • Pectoralis Major (Chest): Main pressing force
  • Deltoids (Front): Shoulder pressing and planche variations
  • Triceps Brachii: Arm extension in all push movements

Secondary

  • Core: Significant engagement in handstand push-ups, wall walks
  • Serratus Anterior: Stabilizes scapula in pushup plus and planche
  • Rotator Cuff: Assists shoulder stability

Stabilizer

  • Forearms and Wrists: Balance and support in handstands and planche
  • Shoulder Stabilizers: Maintain joint integrity under bodyweight stress

üî¥ Push Workout 2

Primary

  • Deltoids (Front & Side): Overhead and incline pressing
  • Pectoralis Major (Chest): Main pressing muscle
  • Triceps Brachii: Arm extension in presses and close-grip bench

Secondary

  • Rear Deltoids: Rear delt flyes target shoulder balance
  • Serratus Anterior: Chest stability, especially in pushup plus
  • Rotator Cuff: Shoulder rotation and health, targeted in rotator cuff ER

Stabilizer

  • Core: Supports trunk in overhead presses and stability work
  • Shoulder Stabilizers: Prevent injury during heavy presses

üü¢ Legs Workout

Primary

  • Gluteus Maximus: Hip extension power
  • Quadriceps: Knee extension during squats and lunges
  • Hamstrings: Hip extension, posterior chain strength
  • Calves (Gastrocnemius & Soleus): Plantar flexion in calf raises

Secondary

  • Adductors: Hip adduction and stabilization (adductor machine)
  • Hip Flexors: Active during reverse lunges and balance work

Stabilizer

  • Core / Abdominals: Bracing, balance during squats, lunges, deadlifts
  • Lower Back: Maintains posture and support under load
